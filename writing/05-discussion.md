# 05 — Discussion

## Role

Lift the findings from "what we measured" to "what it means", generalise the pattern, name
the limits honestly, and point forward. The Discussion argues; it does not re-list results.

## Structure (paragraph by paragraph)

1. **Restate the contribution + conceptual reframing.** Open with what the study provides,
   then elevate it. Pattern: "This study provides [the contribution]. Beyond [empirical
   quantification], it advances understanding by reframing [phenomenon] as [a new
   conceptual lens], rather than [the old framing]."
2. **Generalise the headline pattern.** Re-quantify the main result at the level of a
   general claim, and connect the dimensions: "Overall, this pattern of [finding] is
   primarily driven by [structural cause], rather than [the alternative cause]."
3. **Wider framing (significance / equity / mechanism).** Place the finding in its larger
   stakes — who bears the consequences, what asymmetry or principle it exposes. This is
   where the paper earns its scope.
4. **Limitations, then robustness.** Acknowledge limits by category, then assert that the
   core finding survives them. Pattern: "Although [the framework] provides [value], several
   limitations remain. [Limitation 1]; [limitation 2]… Despite these, the results remain
   robust across [the tested assumptions], suggesting the [finding] reflects a structural
   rather than a modelling artefact." Never end on a limitation.
5. **Forward path.** Close with what should now be done — prescriptive and hopeful.
   Pattern: "Addressing this will require going beyond [the narrow approach]. Future work
   should [integrate X], [incorporate Y], and [link Z]… to translate [potential] into
   [the goal]."

## Tense & voice

- Present tense for claims and interpretation; "should / will require" for the forward path.
- Sparse "we" (per `00`); the conceptual claims are stated objectively.

## Rules baked in

- **Soften absolute claims** and **avoid novelty/exaggeration words** (per `00`) — the
  Discussion is where overreach creeps in. "proves" → "indicates"; "the first to show" →
  delete.
- Limitations must be *specific* (name the assumption, the omitted factor, the data limit),
  not a ritual "further work is needed".
- End with implication and direction, not a summary.

## Do / Don't

- ❌ Re-stating the Results bullet by bullet.
- ❌ Closing on "this study has several limitations."
- ✅ Reframe → generalise → stakes → limits-but-robust → forward path.

## Self-check

Does paragraph 1 reframe the contribution conceptually? Is the headline generalised and
attributed to a structural cause? Are limitations specific and followed by a robustness
claim? Does it end on a forward-looking prescription? Claims softened, no novelty words,
"we" sparse?
