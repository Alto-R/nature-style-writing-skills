# 02 — Abstract

## Role

In ~6 sentences, take a non-specialist from why the problem matters to what you found and
what it means. The abstract must stand completely alone.

## Structure (sentence by sentence)

Write **about six sentences**, one job each:

1. **Context + gap.** Why the topic matters (often a broad societal/scientific frame), then
   the knowledge gap. Pattern: "[Subject] is central to [larger goal], yet [the key
   quantity/feasibility/mechanism] remains poorly [quantified / understood / resolved]."
2. **What you did — "Here we show".** The signature move. Pattern: "Here we show, using
   [scale of evidence: N units and M records/observations], that [headline finding]." The
   scale phrase establishes credibility; the headline goes in the main clause.
3. **Result 1 — quantified, with where.** Pattern: "[Quantified statement, e.g. more than
   half / [x]%], with the [largest/greatest] [effect] in [named subgroups/regions]."
4. **Result 2 — a second dimension or interaction.** Pattern: "Adding [factor] moves [x]% of
   [units] into [worse category]," or another crisp quantified effect.
5. **Result 3 — often counterintuitive, with a qualifier.** Pattern: "Although [a hopeful
   factor] could [improve metric] by about [x]% by [year], this is too small to [close the
   gap] for [the most vulnerable subgroup]."
6. **Implication.** What it means for policy/practice/science, hedged. Pattern: "These
   findings [show/indicate] that [the easy fix] alone is unlikely to [deliver the goal], and
   that [targeted action] is needed."

## Tense & voice

- Present tense for the framing and findings; "Here we show" present.
- Active for the study ("we show"), strategic passive for the problem ("remains poorly
  quantified"). Only **one or two** uses of "we" in the whole abstract.

## Rules baked in (must satisfy while writing)

- **Length ≤ ~200 words** (aim ~150).
- **No references / citations** in the abstract.
- **No undefined acronyms.** Spell out anything you use; if an abbreviation is unavoidable
  (e.g. a well-known goal), expand it in place. Better to avoid acronyms entirely.
- **Include "Here we show"** (or a close equivalent: "Here we report / Here we demonstrate").
- **No emphasis formatting, no bullets** (per `00`).
- Numbers carry units in correct form (`mg L⁻¹`, not `mg/L`; see `04`).

## Common failure (from baseline testing)

Capable drafts overuse "we" — "Here we combine… We find… we evaluate… we estimate". Collapse
these: keep "Here we show…" once, then make the results objective ("The mean decline is
[value]"; "Restoration offsets only about [x]%"). Target ≤ 2 "we" in the abstract.

## Self-check

~6 sentences and ≤ ~200 words? Opens with context→gap? Contains "Here we show" + the scale of
evidence? Three quantified results, each with units and a "where"? Closes with a hedged
implication? Zero references, zero undefined acronyms, ≤ 2 "we"?
